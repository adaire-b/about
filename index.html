ABOUT

Adaire Baltzer

Workflow Designer

I get genuinely excited about solving problems with technology. I always have.

What I love most is using what I know to help someone else, especially if it gives them back time they didn't have before, offers a new perspective, or removes the friction that keeps them from doing their best work.

The project pages show what I built. Here's what they don't show:

  • I don't like broken things. I feel compelled to understand them, fix them, and leave them better than I found them.
  • I'm happiest building at a computer. Hours disappear when I'm untangling a problem or making a messy process just work.
  • People sometimes think I'm some kind of magician. I'm not.
  • I build with AI, and I'm proud of that. It hasn't changed the way I think. It has changed what I'm capable of building.
  • I enjoy showing people what a tool can do. The best part is watching them stop worrying about how it works and start thinking about what it lets them accomplish. If it feels a little like magic, I've probably done my job.

It usually starts with someone saying, "This process is driving us crazy." That's my favorite kind of conversation.

I used to think I had chosen teaching over technology. Looking back, I think I've always been happiest where the two meet.
